## Projects

Below are the key research projects I am currently working on as part of my PhD in TexAI at the University of Bor&aring;s.
Each project connects artificial intelligence with sustainable textile innovation.

---

<div class="card-grid">

  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-tag">Circularity &bull; Computer Vision</div>
    <div class="card-title">AI-Driven Textile Waste Sorting</div>
    <p>
      Developing deep learning models (convolutional and vision-language architectures) to classify textile waste
      by fibre type, blend composition, and quality grade. The goal is to automate and scale textile sorting
      for high-quality circular recycling pathways.
    </p>
    <p><strong>Status:</strong> Active &nbsp;&bull;&nbsp; <strong>Tools:</strong> Python, PyTorch, CLIP</p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-tag">Digital Twin &bull; Process Simulation</div>
    <div class="card-title">Digital Twin of Textile Dyeing &amp; Finishing</div>
    <p>
      Building a virtual model of the dyeing and finishing stage of textile production. The digital twin
      integrates sensor data with AI-based process models to enable real-time monitoring, quality prediction,
      and scenario simulation for resource reduction.
    </p>
    <p><strong>Status:</strong> Active &nbsp;&bull;&nbsp; <strong>Tools:</strong> Python, IoT, simulation frameworks</p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-tag">Vision-Language &bull; Fashion AI</div>
    <div class="card-title">Textile Attribute Understanding with VLMs</div>
    <p>
      Using vision-language models (such as CLIP-based architectures) to automatically extract and classify
      textile attributes including pattern, texture, colour, and material type from product images.
      Applied to fashion search, recommendation, and sustainable design assistance.
    </p>
    <p><strong>Status:</strong> Active &nbsp;&bull;&nbsp; <strong>Tools:</strong> Python, CLIP, Fashionpedia</p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-tag">Data &bull; Platforms</div>
    <div class="card-title">Data Platforms for Circular Textile Value Chains</div>
    <p>
      Investigating the design of interoperable data platforms and digital product passports that connect
      brands, recyclers, and policy-makers along the textile value chain to enable traceable and auditable
      circular flows.
    </p>
    <p><strong>Status:</strong> Exploratory &nbsp;&bull;&nbsp; <strong>Partners:</strong> University of Bor&aring;s TexAI</p>
  </div>

</div>
