## Research overview

I am a PhD student in the **TexAI** programme at the Department of Informatics, University of Bor&aring;s, Sweden.
My research investigates how artificial intelligence can accelerate the transition to circular textile systems
and enable intelligent digital twins of textile products and processes.

---

## Circular economy in textiles

The textile industry is one of the most resource-intensive sectors globally, generating enormous quantities
of fibre waste that ends up in landfill or incineration. A circular economy approach seeks to keep materials
in use as long as possible through reuse, repair, and high-quality recycling.

My work in this area focuses on:

- **AI-driven fibre identification and textile sorting** for automated, high-accuracy separation of textile waste by material composition.
- **Data-driven material flow models** to understand and predict how textiles move through the economy, and where value can be recovered.
- **Decision support tools** that embed circular design principles into early-stage product and process design in the textile industry.
- **Traceability and digital product passports** to enable transparent, data-rich circular value chains.

---

## Digital twin for textile systems

A digital twin is a dynamic virtual model of a physical system, updated in real time with sensor data.
In textiles, digital twins offer transformative potential for sustainable design, process optimisation,
and lifecycle simulation.

My work in this area includes:

- **Digital twins of textile products** to simulate appearance, performance, and end-of-life options without physical prototyping.
- **Process digital twins** for production steps such as dyeing, finishing, and assembly, enabling resource use monitoring and scenario-based optimisation.
- **Virtual sampling pipelines** that reduce the number of physical samples required during product development, cutting both cost and environmental impact.
- **Integration of sensor data and AI models** for real-time monitoring and predictive quality control in textile manufacturing.
